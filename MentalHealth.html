The Problem

Basketball is known for being a game of skill, tactics, and intelligence. It teaches players many important lessons that can be implemented in daily life, such as learning to absorb pain, build discipline, and working hard strategically to achieve smart and effective results. Yet, one major issue that is underlined when it comes to basketball is how also the mindset makes players feel isolated and become emotionally unavailable due to their suppression of their emotions.

The Effects on The Court

Having such negligence and lack of awareness can lead to negative impacts on basketball players socially and personally inside and outside the court, like :

  • - Performance Anxiety
  • - Fear of failing and disappointing others
  • - Experiencing social pressure from players, coaches, parents
  • - Facing an identity loss due to injuries
  • - Developing a sense of hatred towards your own sport

    • The Effects Off the Court

    Off the court, the negative effects follow players, even in their personal life outside basketball in which they experience things like :

  • - Difficulty in expressing emotions
  • - Lack of support for mental health by teams
  • - Experiencing pressure to “ be grateful” for having the opportunity
  • - Having this constant lonely atmosphere, especially while recovering
  • - Developing various mental issues such as ADHD, OCD, or Depression

    • The Antidote

    Thus, moderate but effective solutions can be implemented to change one’s perspective and attitude towards everything around them, consequently imrpoving their mental health, like by :

  • - Understand that strength relies on facing your pain and not hiding it. All athletes, especially basketball players, can be both vulnerable and tough since the two traits harmonize with each other rather than cancelling each other out.
  • - Living everyday life with the acceptance that basketball isn’t your whole life, but rather a part of it. This includes exploring and discovering new passions, interests, and hobbies which can help build relationships which highlight how your value exceeds your stats.
  • - Surround yourself with people who appreciate you for who you truly are and not as a basketball player, as those people will stand with you during the highs and lows.
  • - Make it habit of regularly meeting with someone that could help provide support, clarity, and understanding, like a counselor, mentor or coach. After all, mental health training is a part of a basketball player’s training, and not a separate matter.
